These group discussions grew into the Design to Empower workshops, which were
created with the girls of the Good Shepherd Center at the center of the experience. 
In the spring of 2009, the workshops were finally ready to go and I had permission from
the administration at Good Shepherd Center to introduce the workshops to two of their
residents. I met Kate and Sierra for an hour twice a week for approximately four weeks. 
I began my first session with Kate and Sierra by showing them a variety of zines in
different formats presenting different topics and introduced the workshop exercises. 
Throughout the progression of the workshops, we talked about family, loved ones,
the challenges that brought them to Good Shepherd Center and I tried to carefully usher
them through their moments of doubt and insecurities. At the end of the seventh day,
each girl produced their own zine and each zine tells their story. 

This thesis is not an advertising campaign for women’s rights but rather a collaborative
design initiative that will bring more awareness to the social and political empowerment
of women. Many of the organizations that seek to empower women, lack a strong
presence in the Baltimore community. The services and facilities available to those
that are challenged by social and political issues seem so hidden from potential
investors and the people that could benefit from their services.
